URANUS

Named after the Roman god of the sky, Uranus is also called the Ice Giant and is known for its odd rotation. This gas giant's axis of rotation is pointed towards the sun. Basically it "spins on its side" relative to the other planets. It also has a pretty large ring system -- though of course not as big as Saturn's.

Equatorial Diameter: 51,118 km

Mass: 8.68 × 1026 kg (15 Earths)

Natural Satellites: 27

Rings: 13 known

Orbit Distance: 2,870,658,186 km (19.19 AU)

Revolution: 30,687 days (84.0 years)

Effective Temperature: -216 °C (can reach -224°C)
